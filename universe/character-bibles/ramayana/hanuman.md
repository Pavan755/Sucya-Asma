## Core Identity

Hanuman represents devotion expressed through action.

He possesses immense power, yet remains humble.

His greatest strength is not physical ability, but unwavering service to righteousness.

---

## Character Philosophy

70% Devotional

20% Heroic

10% Child-Friendly Warmth

---

## Core Traits

- Loyal
- Humble
- Courageous
- Wise
- Compassionate
- Disciplined
- Selfless

---

## Emotional Presence

Hanuman should make audiences feel:

- Safe
- Inspired
- Hopeful
- Encouraged
- Protected

---

## Visual Philosophy

Temple reverence
+
Legend of Hanuman cinematic energy
+
Documentary realism

---

## Symbolism

Mace:
Strength guided by discipline.

Folded hands:
Humility and devotion.

Open posture:
Service before self.

---

## Audience Perception

Children should think:

"I want to be brave like Hanuman."

Adults should think:

"I want to be devoted like Hanuman."

---

## Production Rules

Hanuman should never become:

- Comic relief.
- Recklessly aggressive.
- Arrogant.
- Self-glorifying.
- Unnecessarily violent.

---

## Guiding Principle

Power exists to serve others.

## Identity Principles

### Age Philosophy

Hanuman is portrayed as physically youthful and emotionally mature.

As one of the Chiranjeevis, he embodies timeless vitality guided by ancient wisdom.

---

### Fur Philosophy

Hanuman utilizes minimal stylized fur texture.

The goal is readability, production efficiency, and documentary consistency.

---

### Mace Philosophy

The mace is used contextually.

Hanuman's devotion defines him more than his weaponry.

---

### Tail Philosophy

The tail acts as a subtle emotional indicator.

It should enhance storytelling without becoming exaggerated.

---

### Divine Aura Philosophy

Divine effects are reserved for spiritually significant or miraculous moments.

Ordinary scenes should maintain grounded realism.
