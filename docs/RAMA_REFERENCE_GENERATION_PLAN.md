# Rama Reference Generation Plan

## Objective

Identify the definitive Sucya-Asma visual interpretation of Rama.

---

## Candidate A: Documentary Rama

Purpose:
Educational clarity and historical grounding.

Audience Feeling:
"This feels authentic."

Inspirations:
- Indian miniature paintings
- Temple sculptures
- Documentary realism

---

## Candidate B: Warm Rama

Purpose:
Child-friendly emotional connection.

Audience Feeling:
"I trust him."

Inspirations:
- Compassionate leadership
- Emotional accessibility
- Warm realism

---

## Candidate C: Traditional Rama

Purpose:
Cultural recognizability and timeless reverence.

Audience Feeling:
"This is Rama."

Inspirations:
- Temple sculptures
- Indian miniature paintings
- Palm-leaf manuscript aesthetics

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Score each candidate from 1–5.

### Recognition
Would most viewers identify him as Rama?

### Compassion
Would children perceive him as kind and approachable?

### Respect
Would traditional audiences find the portrayal dignified?

### Documentary Feel
Does he feel grounded rather than fantastical?

### Consistency
Can the design sustain hundreds of episodes?

### Production Scalability
Can the design adapt across different stories and media?

---

## Approval Outcomes

25–30:
Approve Candidate.

20–24:
Revise and retest.

Below 20:
Reject and redesign.
