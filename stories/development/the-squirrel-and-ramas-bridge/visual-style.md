# Visual Language Design

## Illustration Style

Sucya-Asma follows an "Ancient Indian Documentary Illustration" style.

### Inspirations

* Amar Chitra Katha
* Indian miniature paintings
* Temple sculptures
* Palm-leaf manuscripts
* Traditional Indian mural aesthetics

### Enhancements

* Cinematic composition
* Environmental depth
* Subtle realism
* Documentary clarity

### Avoid

* Japanese anime aesthetics
* Disney-inspired caricatures
* Hyper-realistic CGI
* Excessive stylization

---

## Character Philosophy

Characters should be recognizable, emotionally expressive, and respectful to their traditional portrayals.

### Divine Characters

* Compassionate
* Calm
* Wise
* Dignified
* Approachable

### Human Characters

* Grounded
* Relatable
* Historically plausible

### Animal Characters

* Naturalistic
* Expressive without becoming cartoonish

---

## Environment Philosophy

Environments should support immersion and historical plausibility.

### Principles

* Ancient Indian aesthetics
* Geographic consistency
* Clear visual storytelling
* Functional environmental design

### Examples

* Ayodhya
* Lanka
* Forest hermitages
* Ocean landscapes

---

## Color Philosophy

### Primary Palette

* Earth browns
* Sand tones
* Muted greens
* Deep blues
* Warm golds

### Emotional Usage

Wonder:

* Cool blues
* Soft sunlight

Compassion:

* Warm ambers
* Gentle highlights

Divinity:

* Subtle golden accents

Reflection:

* Sunrise palettes
* Soft oranges

### Avoid

* Neon saturation
* Constant visual intensity

---

## Motion Philosophy

### Standard Scenes (90%)

Use:

* Parallax movement
* Slow push-ins
* Gentle camera pans
* Environmental animation
* Floating particles
* Cloth movement

### Special Scenes (10%)

Use:

* LTX handcrafted sequences

Reserved for:

* Divine encounters
* Miracles
* Major transformations
* Sacred moments

---

## Divine Representation Guidelines

Divine figures must evoke reverence through presence rather than spectacle.

### Principles

* Compassion before power.
* Stillness before action.
* Warmth before grandeur.
* Grace before intimidation.

### Visual Characteristics

* Subtle lighting enhancements.
* Gentle environmental responses.
* Soft golden highlights when appropriate.
* Calm facial expressions.
* Respectful posture and movement.

### Avoid

* Excessive power effects.
* Aggressive visual exaggeration.
* Overly stylized combat-anime aesthetics.
* Continuous visual spectacle.

Divinity should feel sacred, approachable, and emotionally resonant.

---

## Accessibility Guidelines

Visuals should remain understandable across devices.

### Prioritize

* Clear silhouettes
* Readable compositions
* Moderate pacing
* Strong focal points
* Mobile-friendly framing

### Avoid

* Visual clutter
* Excessive camera motion
* Overly dense scenes

---

## Sucya-Asma Visual Principles

1. Emotion before spectacle.
2. Reverence before entertainment.
3. Presence before power.
4. Clarity before complexity.
5. Historical inspiration before fantasy.
6. Documentary storytelling before action.
7. Children and adults should understand the visuals equally well.
